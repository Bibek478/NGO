Sucessful engagement of PSRWS under the Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM)

Published on: October 28, 2025 | Category: Project Updates

We are thrilled to announce the successful engagement of Swachh Bharat Mission specifically highlighting its contribution to rural household toilet construction in Phansidewa Block

Building a Cleaner Future: Rural Household Toilet Construction by Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society under Swachh Bharat Mission

Introduction:

The Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) launched by the Government of India is a transformative initiative aimed at promoting cleanliness, hygiene, and the construction of toilets to end open defecation. An integral part of this mission has been the participation of NGOs working on the ground. One such committed organization is the Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society (PSRWS), actively working in Phansidewa Block of Darjeeling District, West Bengal.

About the Organization:

Founded in October 2014, Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society is a registered NGO working for rural development and public health improvement. The society has actively engaged in community-level interventions in the areas of sanitation, water supply, solid waste management, and hygiene behavior change. Working Area: Phansidewa Block (rural areas),

- Key Engagement: Swachh Bharat Mission – Gramin (SBM-G)

- Focus: Sustainable sanitation, hygiene awareness, and infrastructure support

Introduction:

The Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) launched by the Government of India is a transformative initiative aimed at promoting cleanliness, hygiene, and the construction of toilets to end open defecation. An integral part of this mission has been the participation of NGOs working on the ground. One such committed organization is the Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society (PSRWS), actively working in Phansidewa Block of Darjeeling District, West Bengal.

Toilet Construction under SBM in Phansidewa Block:

As a supporting agency under SBM, PSRWS has played a crucial role in facilitating the construction of Individual Household Latrines (IHHL) across multiple villages in Phansidewa Block. The NGO has worked in close coordination with the Block Development Office (BDO), Panchayati Raj Institutions (PRIs), and rural households to ensure construction quality and timely execution.

Key Activities by PSRWS in Toilet Construction:

1. Community Mobilization & Awareness:

- Conducted village meetings, house-to-house visits, and awareness camps to encourage families to build and use toilets.

- Explained the health benefits and dignity associated with sanitation, especially to women and marginalized groups.

2. Technical Support & Monitoring: - Provided guidance on design, material quality, and proper construction methods. Regular monitoring of toilet construction to ensure compliance with SBM guidelines.

3. Facilitating Convergence: - Helped beneficiaries avail subsidies under SBM by supporting them with necessary documentation and coordination with government departments.

4. Encouraging Usage Post-Construction: - Promoted behavioral change to ensure toilets are used consistently and maintained hygienically.

Achievements in Phansidewa Block (Highlights):

- Facilitated construction of several hundred household toilets across remote and vulnerable communities.

- Strengthened local capacity through training of masons and community volunteers.

- Actively supported Gram Panchayats in achieving and sustaining ODF status.

Impact on Rural Communities:

- Significant reduction in open defecation in the intervention areas.

- Improved women's safety, privacy, and dignity, especially in tribal and backward villages.

- Created awareness about handwashing, water safety, and environmental hygiene.

- Encouraged community-led sanitation movements that continue beyond infrastructure creation.

Way Forward:

PSRWS is now focusing on the next phase of SBM — supporting villages in achieving ODF Plus by working on:

Solid and liquid waste management

- Toilet retrofitting and repair

- Sustainability of usage and maintenance

- Menstrual hygiene awareness and management

Conclusion:

The work of Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society in promoting rural household toilet construction under the Swachh Bharat Mission is a testament to how grassroots efforts can bring transformative change. By combining awareness, technical guidance, and community engagement, PSRWS is not just building toilets — it is building a culture of cleanliness and dignity in the rural heartland of Phansidewa. As the mission moves toward a more holistic and sustainable sanitation model, the role of dedicated NGOs like PSRWS remains more crucial than ever.

Sucessful engagement of PSRWS under Solid Waste Management(SWM) program

Published on: October 18, 2025 | Category: Project Updates

We are thrilled to announce the successful engagement of Solid Waste Management(SWM) program focusing on its work in Phansidewa, Naxalbari, Matigara, and Kharibari blocks

Introduction:

As India strides toward a cleaner and greener future, Solid Waste Management (SWM) has become a vital component of sustainable rural development. In line with the Government’s vision under the Swachh Bharat Mission (SBM) and related environmental initiatives, Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society (PSRWS) has emerged as a key implementing agency in the Darjeeling district, working diligently across Phansidewa, Naxalbari, Matigara, and Kharibari blocks

About the Organization:

Established in October 2014, is a registered NGO recognized for its impactful work in health, hygiene, sanitation, environmental protection, and rural development. Over the years, PSRWS has partnered with various government departments to support community welfare programs.

Key Engagements Solid Waste Management (SWM):

- Operational Blocks:

- Phansidewa

- Naxalbari

- Matigara

- Kharibari

Solid Waste Management Activities by PSRWS:

As an engaged agency under the Solid Waste Management initiative, PSRWS focuses on building awareness, enabling infrastructure, and promoting environmentally sustainable waste disposal practices at the village and GP level.

Core Activities Include

1. Awareness Generation:

- Conducting community sensitization campaigns on waste segregation at source.

- Use of street plays, IEC materials, and workshops to educate people about the dangers of open dumping and burning of waste.

2. Waste Segregation & Collection Support:

- Promoting segregation of biodegradable and non-biodegradable waste in households.

- Supporting door-to-door waste collection models through coordination with local panchayats.

3. Capacity Building of Local Workers:

- Training waste collectors, SHGs, and GP staff on safe handling, composting, and processing techniques.

- Encouraging involvement of youth and women’s groups in livelihood generation through recycling.

4. Support in Waste Disposal Infrastructure:

- Facilitating establishment of compost pits, vermicomposting units, and waste storage sheds.

- Promoting home composting and use of organic waste for farming.

5. Monitoring and Evaluation:

- Routine field visits to assess waste management practices.

- Submitting reports to local government bodies and suggesting actionable improvements.

Impact in the Field:

- Reduction in open dumping and burning of waste in several rural areas.

- Increase in community participation and ownership of cleanliness efforts.

- Establishment of model villages demonstrating proper SWM practices.

- Integration of SWM with health, hygiene, and water safety awareness programs.

Vision Ahead:

PSRWS envisions expanding its SWM efforts by:

- Linking solid waste efforts with MGNREGA and SHG-based livelihood models.

- Promoting plastic waste reduction and reuse through eco-clubs and schools.

- Supporting GP-level Waste Management Action Plans for sustainability.

- Advocating for policy-level support for rural waste workers and waste entrepreneurs.

Conclusion:

With dedicated on-ground teams and a people-first approach, Phansidewa Social & Rural Welfare Society is playing a pioneering role in transforming rural sanitation and waste management practices. Their work in Phansidewa, Naxalbari, Matigara, and Kharibari blocks underlines how community engagement, awareness, and infrastructure support can turn villages into models of sustainable living. As India moves towards a Swachh and Swasth Bharat, the role of grassroots agencies like PSRWS will continue to be essential in creating long-term, eco-friendly, and health-oriented waste management systems.
